Overview

Project Overview

This project analyzes four years (2021–2024) of large-scale e-commerce data (~1M+ rows) to help business stakeholders understand revenue trends, customer behavior, operational bottlenecks, and growth opportunities across regions, categories, and payment channels.

The objective was to build an executive-ready Power BI dashboard that enables leadership to track performance, identify key sales drivers, and make confident, data-driven decisions at scale.

Dataset

1M+ transaction records across orders, revenue, profit, returns — 2021 to 2024

Tools Used

Power BI · DAX · Power Query · Star Schema · ETL Pipeline

Best Result

25+ DAX measures · Revenue from ₹6.2B to ₹14.4B tracked across 4 years

Business Value

Executive KPI dashboard · state-level targeting · cancellation impact analysis

Data

Dataset & Features

Transaction-level e-commerce data capturing orders, revenue, profit, returns, and cancellations across 4 years — spanning geography, product category, order lifecycle, and payment method dimensions.

Key Features

Geography (state-level) Product category & sub-category Order lifecycle & cancellations Payment method (UPI, cards, wallets) Time dimensions (Year, Month) Revenue, profit & return metrics

Data Challenges

Identifying true revenue drivers at scale Impact of cancellations & returns on profit YoY growth across 4-year span Non-technical stakeholder accessibility

Power Query was used for all data cleaning, transformation, and validation. A clean star schema was designed before building any DAX measures to ensure model performance and scalability at 1M+ row scale.

Insights

Key Insights

Findings from EDA and dashboard exploration that inform investment decision-making.

Revenue grew from ₹6.2B to ₹14.4B over four years — a 132% increase indicating sustained business growth.

Electronics consistently emerged as the highest revenue-generating category across all four years.

Maharashtra & Karnataka are the most reliable demand centers — highest and most consistent revenue.

UPI dominates digital payments with strong adoption growth — most popular and fastest-growing payment method.

Returns and cancellations reduce revenue in specific states and months — regional operational gaps identified.

The AI-style sales driver tree reveals multi-level revenue attribution from state → category → payment method.
